The question isn't whether AI will take software jobs. It's whether engineers who use AI effectively will replace those who don't.

This reframe matters. It shifts the question from threat to positioning — from something that happens to you to something you can act on.

## The Leverage Argument

Every major tool in the history of engineering has been a form of leverage. Higher-level languages meant you could express more with less. Version control meant you could build without fear of losing work. Frameworks meant you didn't reinvent infrastructure on every project.

AI is the same thing, one order of magnitude larger.

An engineer using AI well doesn't become less capable — they become capable of more. The surface area of what one person can ship expands significantly. This is multiplication, not substitution.

## What Gets Multiplied

Not everything scales equally. The parts that AI accelerates most:

- **Boilerplate generation.** The scaffolding that takes time but requires little judgment.
- **First drafts.** The hard part of starting — AI can give you something to react to.
- **Pattern application.** If you've solved it before, AI can apply the pattern faster than you can type it.

What doesn't get automatically multiplied:

- **Problem framing.** Knowing what to build is harder than building it.
- **Judgment calls.** Trade-offs that require context and values, not pattern matching.
- **Quality bar.** Recognizing when generated output is subtly wrong.

## The Engineer's New Role

The role becomes more curatorial and directorial. You're not just writing code — you're reviewing it, steering it, asking better questions of it. The judgment layer becomes more central, not less.

This is actually closer to how great senior engineers have always worked. They don't type everything themselves. They understand deeply, direct precisely, and review critically.

AI doesn't obsolete this. It makes it the default.

## The Practical Implication

Use the tools. Use them aggressively. But don't let them become a crutch that atrophies your understanding of what's happening underneath. The multiplier only works if there's something to multiply.

Stay sharp on the fundamentals. AI handles the what. You need to own the why.
